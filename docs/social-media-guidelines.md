# Social Media & LinkedIn Guidelines

**For:** Founders, team members, and advisors
**Purpose:** Build brand awareness, drive organic traffic, and strengthen domain authority for aloraops.ai

---

## Brand Voice on Social

**Authoritative but practical.** We speak from operational experience, not theory. We avoid hype, buzzwords, and "disruption" language.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Share specific operational insights | Use generic AI hype ("revolutionary", "game-changing") |
| Describe real supply chain problems | Overpromise or make unverifiable claims |
| Be direct and concise | Write corporate jargon |
| Acknowledge complexity | Oversimplify ("just use AI") |
| Show expertise through specifics | Name-drop competitors negatively |

**Tone:** The person reading should think "this person has actually dealt with this problem" — not "this person is trying to sell me something."

## Content Pillars

### 1. Operational Insights (40%)
Real problems in supply chain execution. These posts establish credibility.

**Examples:**
- "The real cost of a late PO confirmation isn't the part — it's the 3 BOMs that can't build, the 2 customer orders that slip, and the expedite fee that eats margin."
- "Your ERP shows PO status. It doesn't show that your supplier confirmed 3 of 6 lines, went silent on 2, and changed the price on 1."
- "We track ~200 signals per supplier relationship. Most procurement teams track 3: price, delivery date, quantity."

### 2. Industry Perspectives (25%)
Observations about specific industries we serve. These attract the right audience.

**Examples:**
- "Medical device procurement teams spend 40% of their time managing regulatory cascades from part changes. The change itself takes a day. The compliance ripple takes months."
- "In defense manufacturing, a $200 connector can block a $2M contract milestone. Standard risk scoring doesn't capture that."

### 3. Product Updates (20%)
What we're building and why. Not feature announcements — the problem they solve.

**Examples:**
- "We just shipped cascade-aware risk scoring. When a motor controller is delayed, the score now reflects impact on every assembly downstream — not just the one PO line."
- "New: vendor scoring at the part-category level. A supplier who delivers standard parts on time but struggles with custom machined parts now shows both patterns."

### 4. Founder Stories (15%)
Personal journey, lessons learned, hiring, culture. These humanize the brand.

**Examples:**
- "Three months ago I sat with a procurement manager who had 847 open PO lines. She knew 12 were critical. The other 835? 'I check when I can.' That conversation shaped what we built."
- "Why we chose to build for hardware manufacturers, not retail or logistics."

## Posting Guidelines

### Frequency
- **Founders:** 2-3 posts per week
- **Team members:** 1-2 posts per week (encouraged, not required)
- **Advisors:** See advisor section below

### Post Structure
Every post should follow this pattern:

1. **Hook** (1-2 lines) — Something that makes the reader stop scrolling. A surprising stat, a provocative observation, or a relatable frustration.
2. **Context** (2-4 lines) — Set the scene. What's the actual situation?
3. **Insight** (2-4 lines) — Your perspective. What most people miss. What you've learned.
4. **Engagement prompt** (1 line) — A question that invites comments. Not generic ("What do you think?") — specific ("What's the signal you wish your ERP captured?").

### Example Post
```
The most expensive part in your BOM isn't what you think.

It's the $3 capacitor with a 16-week lead time from a single
qualified source. When it slips 2 weeks, three builds can't ship.
Revenue at risk: $1.2M.

Your ERP shows it as "on order." Alora shows it as Critical —
because it connects the part to every BOM and customer order
it touches.

What's the part in your supply chain that punches above its weight?
```

## Advisor Guidelines

Advisors bring credibility and reach. These guidelines help them mention Alora naturally without it feeling forced.

### General Approach
- **Lead with expertise, not promotion.** Advisors should post about their domain expertise. Alora is mentioned when relevant — not in every post.
- **Frequency:** Mention Alora in ~1 of every 4 posts (when it fits naturally)
- **Tone:** "I'm advising this company because I believe in the problem they're solving" — not "Use Alora!"

### Good Examples
- "One of the companies I advise, @Alora, is tackling this exact problem — turning supplier signals into actionable risk scores at the BOM level."
- "I've spent 20 years in defense procurement. The hardest part isn't finding suppliers — it's knowing which open POs actually threaten your program milestones. That's what drew me to advise Alora."
- Post about a general supply chain topic, then in comments: "This is exactly the kind of problem Alora is building for. I'm an advisor and seeing interesting progress here."

### What to Avoid
- Don't use marketing language ("revolutionary", "game-changing", "disruptive")
- Don't make performance claims without data ("saves 50% of time")
- Don't tag Alora in every post — it dilutes impact
- Don't copy-paste company messaging — use your own words

## Editing Previous LinkedIn Posts

If you have existing LinkedIn posts about supply chain, manufacturing, or procurement topics, consider adding links to the new landing pages:

### How to Edit
1. Find the post on LinkedIn
2. Click the "..." menu → "Edit post"
3. Add a relevant link at the end: "Related: I wrote more about this approach here → [URL]"
4. Save

### Which Posts to Edit
- Posts about PO management → add link to `/solutions/po-risk-tracking`
- Posts about build planning or BOM visibility → add link to `/solutions/build-readiness`
- Posts about supplier evaluation → add link to `/solutions/vendor-scoring`
- Posts about specific industries → add link to relevant industry page

**Don't overdo it.** Edit 3-5 high-performing posts, not every post you've ever written.

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do
- Share real numbers and specifics (anonymized if needed)
- Engage with comments on your posts (respond within 24 hours)
- Comment on other people's supply chain posts (adds visibility)
- Use 2-3 relevant hashtags: #SupplyChain #Manufacturing #Procurement #AI
- Post during business hours (Tue-Thu, 8-10am tends to perform best)

### Don't
- Auto-post the same content to multiple platforms
- Use engagement bait ("Like if you agree!")
- Post walls of text without line breaks
- Share confidential customer information
- Criticize specific competitors by name
- Use stock photos — if you need an image, use screenshots, diagrams, or data visualizations
